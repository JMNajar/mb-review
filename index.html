REGULATORY STATUS

FDA Classification of Methylene Blue

The strongest regulatory reading is that methylene blue is a poor fit for the dietary-supplement pathway on its own — and is more securely regulated as a drug when marketed with therapeutic or disease-related claims. Understanding this is critical before product launch.

ClassificationStatusWhat It Means
Approved Prescription Drug CONFIRMED ProvayBlue approved 2016 (NDA 204630). Generic approved Dec 2023. Rx-only IV for acquired methemoglobinemia.
Lawful Dietary Supplement HIGH RISK MB is not obviously a vitamin, mineral, botanical, or amino acid. The 201(ff)(3)(B) approved-drug exclusion likely blocks supplement status. No FDA methylene-blue-specific supplement determination exists.
Unapproved Drug (with disease claims) ENFORCEMENT RISK FDA issued 2020 warning letters against firms selling MB with COVID-19 claims. DOJ/FDA-OCI sentenced a couple for selling unapproved MB products.
OTC Drug / Monograph NOT FOUND MB does not appear in FDA's OTC Monograph ingredient list. No approved OTC MB product identified.
Vitamin C (supplement anchor) CLEAR Definitively a dietary ingredient under DSHEA. No classification risk. Anchors the product in supplement territory.

Key FDA Milestones

2011
FDA Drug Safety Communication: serious serotonin syndrome risk when MB is combined with serotonergic psychiatric drugs.
2016
FDA approves ProvayBlue (NDA 204630) — first FDA-approved treatment for acquired methemoglobinemia.
2020
FDA warning letters issued against North Isle Wellness Center and Center for Wellness for selling MB with COVID-19 claims as unapproved new drugs.
2023
FDA approves first generic methylene blue injection (ANDA 215636).
2024
FDA updates MB label with boxed serotonin-syndrome warning. Final guidance on NDI notification procedures issued.
2026
FDA public meeting on dietary supplement ingredient scope (March 2026). MB remains in regulatory gray zone for supplement market.
